Key observations:
- Cosine s=0.02 achieves the best mean RMSE (0.1028) with the lowest variance (Std=0.0164),
  making it the most consistent and accurate schedule across all 8 tested
- Quadratic is a close second (0.1147, Std=0.0326) — previously ranked best before cosine
  variants were included
- Cosine s=0.001 (0.1177) slots just below quadratic, outperforming standard cosine by a
  large margin, suggesting the smaller offset s helps in this domain
- Sigmoid (0.1357) remains competitive; Cosine s=0.10 (0.1628) and Geometric (0.1635)
  perform similarly in mean RMSE but Cosine s=0.10 has far higher variance (Std=0.0947),
  driven by two outlier runs (result_06: 0.3866, result_10: 0.2926)
- Standard Cosine (0.1692) is mid-tier; the tuned s variants clearly improve on it
- Linear remains worst (0.2397) with very high variance (Std=0.1766)
- The s parameter in cosine schedules spans s=0.001, s=0.02, s=0.10 in these experiments;
  at 100 epochs the sweet spot is s=0.02 — s=0.10 underperforms here, likely because its
  larger offset requires more training to converge (confirmed by the 300-epoch results)
